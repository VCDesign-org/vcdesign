# Chapters — Why This Unit Exists

## Why a "Chapter" Is Necessary

The moment when value is lost is not a moment of completion,
nor is it a system failure.

It happens quietly,
during everyday operations,
without being explicitly observed by anyone.

At first, we believed these breakdowns occurred because
designs were incomplete,
or documentation was insufficient.

So we added more specifications.
We wrote more documents.

Yet the same discontinuities kept repeating.

The problem was not the amount of information.
The problem was the **unit that design itself was using**.

---

## Why Phases and Versions Were Not Enough

Software development has phases.
It has versions.

They are effective for tracking progress
and recording changes.

But over time, a different discomfort emerged.

- Why was this judgment made at that moment?
- As whose responsibility was it accepted?
- When, and where, was that responsibility handed off next?

Neither phases nor versions record
the moments when human judgment is accepted,
carried,
and eventually released.

Time certainly passes.
But that passage does not remain in the design.

---

## What a Chapter Represents

A Chapter is not a development phase.
It is not a version boundary.

A Chapter is a **time-based design unit** that captures:

- When judgments are implicitly rewritten
- When responsibility silently shifts
- When meaning erodes without explicit decisions

Chapters exist to make these invisible transitions
observable,
discussable,
and designable.

This is why VCDesign treats Chapters
as a first-class design unit.
