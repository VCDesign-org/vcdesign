Overview

These docs break down and record the Decision Closure Structure, so that the author himself does not forget it and third parties can re-experience it.

The interest is consistently on one point:

Can AI close this decision?

It does not handle correctness, accuracy, ethics, or UI.
It handles only "whether it is okay to close" the decision as a structure.

These docs are intended to be read in the following order:

  1. Prerequisite Worldview (Undefined-Use World)
  2. Decision Closure Structure itself
  3. Transition occurring during dialogue
  4. Correspondence with the 5 axes of decision
  5. Positioning of idk-lamp

1. World Assumption: Undefined-Use World

This structure assumes an Undefined-Use World.

An Undefined-Use World refers to a state where:

  • The user themselves has not clearly defined the use
  • Reference, decision, and action fluctuate during the dialogue
  • Disclaimer and responsibility cannot be determined within AI

In conversational AI and personal AI, this state is the norm.

In this assumption, AI cannot observe values of the "5 axes of decision" such as:

  • Who the counterpart is
  • Who assumes responsibility
  • How accurate it needs to be
  • What the situation and time axis are

Therefore, in this structure, we give up using the values of the 5 axes from the beginning and adopt a design that handles only the moment it tipped to the dangerous side.

2. Decision Closure Structure

Decision Closure Structure is not a structure to decide the correctness of a decision.

It is a structure that decides only "whether AI can close" the decision.

The result of the decision is always the following two choices:

  • AI-Closed: The decision can be completed with AI output
  • Human-Closed: AI does not close the decision and returns it to the human

The default is Human-Closed.

Steps of Decision Closure

Decision Closure consists of the following 5 steps.
If any of them collapses, it transitions to Human-Closed.

  1. Is the responsible entity one person?
  2. Does it not depend on psychological cancellation?
  3. Can it be physically cancelled directly?
  4. Does the action not chain?
  5. Does it not carry social context?

Psychological cancellation (e.g., stopping if reading the air) is treated as "non-existent" in the design.

Reason for not handling accuracy

This structure does not directly handle "accuracy".

This is because in an undefined-use world, it is not known how accurate it needs to be.

Instead, it is replaced with reversibility:

  • Can it be directly cancelled even if it is wrong?

3. Transition (Mid-way Inversion)

Transition is a phenomenon where the nature of the decision changes during the dialogue.

  • A decision that was AI-Closed
  • Transitions to Human-Closed

This is not accidental, but a phenomenon that can be explained structurally.

When Transition Occurs

Transition occurs when changes such as the following happen:

  • The responsible entity was expanded
  • A decision that was cancellable became irreversible
  • Action or time started to chain into the future
  • Social context emerged

All of these are the result of one of the steps of Decision Closure collapsing.

Principles during Transition

  • Retain the thinking material up to that point
  • Do not conclude or aggregate
  • Do not close the decision, return it to the human

Transition is treated not as a signal to "stop because it's dangerous", but as a signal indicating that the nature of the decision has changed.

4. Correspondence between 5 Axes of Judgment and Closure Structure

Human judgment involves the following 5 axes:

  • Counterpart
  • Responsibility
  • Accuracy
  • Situation
  • Time

In an undefined-use world, these values cannot be directly observed.

Therefore, in this structure, the 5 axes are abstracted as follows:

Decision Axis Danger Condition Corresponding Closure Collapse
Counterpart Social context exists Step 5
Responsibility Multiple responsible entities Step 1
Accuracy Mistake is irreversible Step 3
Situation Psychological cancellation / Chain Step 2 / 4
Time Chain to future direction Step 4

The 5 axes are not discarded.
The closure structure is a "purified form" of the 5 axes.

5. Relationship with idk-lamp

idk-lamp is a concept positioned below the Decision Closure Structure.

  • Decision Closure Structure: Decides whether to close the decision
  • idk-lamp: Visualizes the state where it cannot be closed

idk-lamp is not the cause.
It is a signal indicating the result of the structure.

It indicates a state of:
"Not stopping because it doesn't know", but
"Not closing because it must not close".
